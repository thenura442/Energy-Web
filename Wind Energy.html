Wind Energy

What is Wind Eneryg?
The process of using the wind to generate mechanical power or electricity is known as wind power or wind energy. Wind turbines transform the wind's kinetic energy into mechanical energy. This mechanical power can be employed for specialized tasks such as grinding grain or pumping water or transformed into electricity by a generator and also wind turbines can provide energy for onsite use as well as for export for sale. The energy needs will determine the size of the turbine.
Uses of Wind Energy
Wind energy is a renewable energy source. It is non-polluting, inexhaustible, and helps to limit the usage of fossil fuels, which are the source of greenhouse gases that contribute to global warming. Furthermore, wind energy is a "native" energy because it is found almost everywhere on the planet, helping to reduce energy imports while providing income and local jobs. Because of this generating power from wind energy and putting it to good use contributes to long-term sustainability.
  • Generating electricity
  • Pumping water
  • Reducing carbon footprint
  • Powering cargo ships
  • Wind powered vehicles
Pros and Cons of Wind Energy
With increasing awareness of environmental issues across the planet, including climate change, more and more industries and individuals are looking toward renewable, clean energy sources to provide them with the power they need. Wind energy is one of these energy sources, and is quickly growing in popularity across the United States and the world. And as with any source of energy, wind energy has its advantages and disadvantages.
